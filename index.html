About Steve

A candidate whose record already reflects the kind of service he is promising.

Steve Edin is a dedicated Marysville resident running for City Council to keep local government grounded, approachable, and accountable. He believes city leadership should be visible in the community, responsive to residents, and focused on the real issues families talk about every day.

Because he is running as an independent, Steve is not affiliated with a political party. His campaign is centered on Marysville itself: preserving community character, supporting safe and well-run public services, and making sure residents feel heard before major decisions are made.

  • Dedicated resident with a community-first campaign.
  • Independent candidate focused on local priorities instead of party talking points.
  • Committed to clear communication, practical solutions, and respectful leadership.

Local Achievement

Saving the Marysville water tower became a clear example of community preservation in action.

One of Steve's most visible local contributions was helping save the Marysville water tower, a familiar landmark that carries history, identity, and civic pride for many residents.

The result mattered because details mattered: the green lid, green legs, and white tank helped preserve the look people recognize and associate with Marysville. That effort showed what can happen when residents speak up and local leaders stay attentive to what a place means to the community.

  • Protected a recognizable Marysville landmark.
  • Honored the community's preferred green-and-white tower design.
  • Demonstrated that local stewardship should shape public decisions.
